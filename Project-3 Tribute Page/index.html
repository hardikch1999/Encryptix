Field Marshal Sam Hormusji Framji Jamshedji Manekshaw

Honoring Field Marshal Sam Bahadur: The Legendary Leader Who Shaped India's Military History.

Biography

Field Marshal Sam Hormusji Framji Jamshedji Manekshaw, affectionately known as Sam Bahadur, was born on April 3, 1914, in Amritsar, Punjab, during the British Raj. He hailed from a Parsi family and grew up with values of discipline, courage, and service. Sam Bahadur's illustrious military career began when he joined the Indian Army in 1934, graduating from the Indian Military Academy with the first batch. His strategic acumen and leadership were prominently displayed during World War II, where he earned recognition for his bravery in Burma. However, it was during the Indo-Pakistani War of 1971 that he cemented his place in history. As the Chief of Army Staff, his decisive and brilliant strategies led to the swift and resounding victory that resulted in the creation of Bangladesh. Field Marshal Manekshaw's legacy extends beyond his military achievements; he is remembered for his wit, wisdom, and unflinching commitment to the nation. His life is a testament to the highest ideals of service, bravery, and leadership, making him a true hero and a source of inspiration for generations to come
